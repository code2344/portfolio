    • Year 9 Program

     

    Year 9 Program was thoughtfully designed to align with the foundational Purpose and Concerns. Grounded in Quaker principles, the program seeks to nurture Identity, Belonging, and a Connection to Country, while immersing students in the natural world.

    By integrating these core elements, the program encourages students to explore their intrinsic worth and reflect on their place within their community and the environment. Through intentional engagement with nature and a focus on simplicity and stewardship, the program fosters a deeper appreciation for the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of living in harmony with the Earth.

    This holistic approach ensures that the Year 9 Program not only addresses the academic and personal development of students but also instils values that inspire them to contribute positively to their communities and the wider world.

     

     

    Courage Reflection - My Story

    Reflecting on the various parts of your Childhood can be enjoyable, challenging and sometimes unearth things or memories you have forgotten. Reflecting on your childhood can offer several benefits, both personal and psychological. 

    Here are some of the advantages:

    Self-awareness, recognising patterns and habits, evaluating relationships, highlighting personal growth, developing empathy and understanding, examining how you coped with challenges, expressing gratitude, highlighting strengths and assisting your storytelling skills.

     

     

    Community Reflection - The Calling

    Waking up early to see the sunrise, and challenge ourselves to a 12 km walk, was an opportunity to move on from the past, and let go of something we do not want to be part of our future.

     

     

    Compassion Reflection - The Departure

    Sitting down with your family and talking about your childhood is a great way to think retrospectively about your childhood and some of the amazing memories you may have forgotten. Seeing how your coming into this world changed their life for the better and listening to their stories is special. 

    Also making the most of the opportunity to show true gratitude to your families is potentially something you may not have done otherwise. Expressing your gratitude is proven to improve your Mental Health and Physical Health, Enhance your Relationships and increase your overall Happiness, resilience and self-esteem.

    Consideration Reflection - Service

    Service develops empathy and teamwork, instils a sense of responsibility, builds character and contributes to community betterment. Recognising our ability to do things for others, using our gifts and talents helps us appreciate our blessings and understand more about the needs of others.

    Connection and Camp Reflection - Abyss / Journey

    The culminating experience within the Year 9 Program is the final Abyss and Journey. 

    Both of these aspects will challenge our students, physically, socially and emotionally, with the knowledge that completing these has many potential benefits.

    The Abyss is a solo camp with no communication or technology. Students are required to be self-reliant and independent - they need to make decisions, solve problems, and manage their time and resources independently, fostering a sense of empowerment and self-confidence.

    Camping without communication or distraction allows them to connect with nature, they can immerse themselves and experience its beauty and serenity. This can promote a deeper appreciation for the natural world, enhance mindfulness, and provide a much-needed break from the constant stimulation of technology. They must rely on their problem-solving skills and creativity to overcome obstacles. This can enhance their critical thinking, decision-making abilities, and resourcefulness.
