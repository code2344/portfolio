    • Abyss / Journey

    The purpose of this event is to challenge students and support them to move towards greater self sufficiency. It aims to immerse students in nature with an opportunity to recognise simplicity, build resilience and community and celebrate with peers. Students have the opportunity to recognise something greater than themselves. 

    Key aspects:

  • attend and actively engage in the opportunities offered on your journey and solo event.
  • reflect on your experiences this year and how you will act on any decisions you made whilst participating in these events. 

    • Think back 12 months and you first heard about this experience? What was your initial reaction? Which elements excited you? Which ones did your feel the most concern about? 

    Often anticipation, self-doubt and worry - leading into some anxiety can offer occur when we have some unknown. How do you feel that you deal/manage this? Share a time when you were worried about something and it didn’t eventually end up being as bad as possible?

    Leading into Years 10/11/12, you are likely to experience exam and study stress, managing this will be key to success. What strategies would you employ (use) to ensure you can focus on the task at hand and not let negative thoughts consume you?

    The Abyss aspect is extremely challenging, as we all crave social connection and also live in a world full of consistent entertainment through various means of technology. Being self-sufficient, reflecting and being in nature is so good for our holistic health. What aspects did you find hardest about the Abyss / Solo?

    Extended time away from family, from basic amenities like hot showers and flushing toilets is challenging. No doubt you would have been looking forward to your bed on return. Also challenging is the daily physical requirements to walk, carry packs, prepare food and sleep in on roll mats. You also might have found something else challenging - the group dynamic, feeling unwell/injury etc. Highlight the biggest challenge you faced throughout the Journey, what you learnt from it and whether you believe that pushing through / overcoming it will serve you well in the future.
